Vocabulary

  • Kinetic energy is the energy of motion (Joules)
  • Temperature is the average kinetic energy of molecules.
  • Thermal energy is energy due to temperature and the number of molecules.
  • Heat is the transfer of thermal energy from hot to cold.
  • Thermal equilibrium is when objects in contact reach the same temperature.
  • Thermal expansion is molecules moving further apart as a result of increased temperature.
  • Thermal conductor allows heat energy to transfer easily.
  • Thermal insulator traps heat energy and is slow to transfer it.

7.1 Introduction to Heat

Temperature is a scale for measuring the average kinetic energy of a group of molecules. There are multiple scales for temperature including Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin.
