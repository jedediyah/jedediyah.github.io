Numerical Computing and Data Science

Overview

This course introduces concepts and consequences of data technologies at an intersection of science and humanities. We approach science and engineering from a computational modeling perspective where computers are our primary tool for gaining insights into phenomena. We will leverage a data science process as a guide for both building and for critically analyzing data science projects.

Before anyone can begin to analyze or build models with data, that data needs to be collected and organized. Wrangling data is a messy process that requires technical skill and determination. We will work with spreadsheets, MATLAB/Octave, JavaScript, and Python to explore varieties of data and fundamentals of modeling with data. No previous coding experience is required.

This course involves much more than coding. There will be several reading assignments and we will examine the history and ethics of data science pipelines, from the extraction of natural resources to the deployment of models into society. Additionally, we will cover elementary concepts of linear algebra, dynamical systems, probability, graph theory, and more. As the year progresses, we will shift from computation to physics simulations to statistical modeling to machine learning. We will develop our broad interests in science, coding, modeling, and sociotechnical ethics.
